CHAPTER 30: THE FALL OF THE PATRIARCH
The silence in the study was absolute, broken only by the hum of the high-security servers that were now under Seo-Jun’s command. President Kim looked at the abdication papers, then up at the two predators he had raised. He didn't look afraid; he looked amused.
President Kim "You’ve played the game well, Seo-Jun. And you, Hana... I taught you to be a shadow, yet here you are standing in the light. But tell me, 'Prince'—do you really trust the woman who was sent to kill you an hour ago?"
He leaned forward, his voice a venomous whisper aimed directly at the crack in their alliance.
President Kim "She’s a mercenary. She didn't choose you because of 'loyalty.' She chose you because you were the highest bidder. What happens when the next auditor comes along? Or when she decides that a Queen doesn't actually need a King to hold the pen?"
** CLICK — SAFETY DISENGAGED **
Hana didn't flinch. She stepped closer to Seo-Jun, her hand resting not on her weapon, but on his shoulder. It was a visual declaration that the President’s psychological games were failing.
Hana "Nice try, Dad. You spent twenty years teaching me how to spot a lie. Your words are just empty static. I don't trust him because of a 'bid.' I trust him because he’s the only one in this house who didn't treat me like a piece of hardware."
Seo-Jun "Enough, Father. The Audit found more than just your offshore accounts. It found the records of my mother’s 'accident.' The same accident you used to bring Hana into this house as a replacement. The game is over. Sign... or the Audit results go to the Prosecutor’s Office in five minutes."
STATUS: THE FINAL SIGNATURE

The President realizes the leverage is absolute. The murder of Seo-Jun's mother is the "Ghost in the Machine" that destroys his legacy forever. He has no moves left.

** SCRATCH — PEN ON PAPER **
With a trembling hand, the Patriarch of the Kim Group scribbled his name. The empire had officially changed hands. The President stood up, his posture suddenly frail, and walked toward the balcony without looking back.
President Kim "The throne is yours. But remember... a throne built on an audit is never stable. Sleep with one eye open, 'Son.' Your Queen is sharper than your crown."
As the old man exited the room under guard, Seo-Jun turned to Hana. The red light was gone, replaced by the cold, white glow of the moon. They were alone in the center of power.
Hana "So... Your Majesty. What’s our first order of business?"
Seo-Jun "The Board is still waiting in the dining hall. Let’s go tell them the Audit is over... and the Clean-up has just begun."
