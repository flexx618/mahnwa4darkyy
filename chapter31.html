CHAPTER 31: THE INHERITANCE OF ASHES
The atmosphere in the dining hall was suffocating. Sheffie, the once untouchable 'Architect' of the Board, stared at the stack of documents before her as if they were her own death warrant. Her hands, usually adorned with diamonds, were now trembling as she smoothed the paper she had just signed.
Sheffie "I’ve signed it all, Seo-Jun. The board shares, the offshore holdings... everything. Now, burn those records. If the news about Rhea’s mother ever leaks, the Kim Group will be gutted by the public."
Seo-Jun picked up the folder. He didn't look at the signed contracts. Instead, he pulled out an old, weathered Diary and a cracked Dashcam SD card—the final pieces of a fifteen-year-old puzzle.
Seo-Jun "You think I want your shares, Sheffie? I already have the company. What I wanted was the truth. This diary details every meeting you had with the Board. "
** SIRENS WAILING IN THE DISTANCE **
The color drained from Sheffie’s face. The Board members scrambled to stand, but the doors burst open. It wasn't just Hana's guards this time—it was the Special Investigation Unit, led by a prosecutor who had been waiting for this data for a decade.
Hana "The Audit is over. But your trial is just beginning. Every signature you just put on those papers is a confession of your corruption. And every word in that diary is a nail in your coffin."
VERDICT: TERMINATION

The leverage is no longer a secret—it’s public record. By handing over the Diary and the Dashcam footage, Seo-Jun has chosen justice over blackmail. Sheffie and the Board are being removed by the law they thought they owned.

Police officers swarmed the table. Handcuffs clicked shut over expensive watches. Sheffie screamed, a jagged, desperate sound, as she was dragged toward the exit. The 'Architect' was finally being taken to a cell she couldn't design her way out of.
Seo-Jun "Take them. All of them. I want the world to see the faces of the people who thought they could murder their way to the top."
As the room emptied of the noise and the filth of the old regime, Hana walked over to Seo-Jun. She looked at the empty SD card reader. The weight of the past seemed to lift from the room.
