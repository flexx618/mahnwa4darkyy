CHAPTER 24: THE BOARD'S EXECUTION
KIM MANSION // THE DINING HALL // THE RECKONING
Gobo slammed the leather-bound diary onto the table. "500 million won, Shefiee. For 'Disposal Services.' My father didn't just clean the blood; he kept the bill."
Shefiee (Voice trembling) "A diary is just ink. It’s a story, nothing more. You have no proof that I—"
Seo-Jun "We have more than ink, Chairperson. We have a ghost."
Seo-Jun tapped a command into his tablet. The massive 4K monitor on the wall flickered. It wasn't the washroom this time. It was a grainy, night-vision dashcam feed from thirteen years ago. The sound of rain hissed through the speakers, followed by a sudden, metallic SNAP.
** STATIC — SCREAM — CRASH **
EVIDENCE: RECOVERED DASHCAM 001

The audio is crystal clear. A woman's voice—Shefiee's sister—begging for the brakes to work. Then, a final, chilling call received on the car's Bluetooth. "Goodbye, sister. The Board doesn't like loose ends," Shefiee’s voice echoes through the dining hall.

Shefiee collapsed into her chair, her face a mask of ashen terror. The combination of the diary's financial trail and the dashcam's audio was a death sentence. Her pride didn't just break; it evaporated.
Seo-Jun "Sign the papers, Shefiee. Not just for your resignation... but for the full transfer of the Board's voting rights to the Kim Bloodline. But you aren't the only one who needs to sign."
President Kim leaned back, a shark-like grin on his face. "Shefiee has already summoned them. They think they're here to celebrate my downfall. They think they're here to divide the spoils."
** DING — DONG — DOOM **
The heavy doorbell rang, echoing through the foyer. The rest of the Board—Min, Choi, and Park—had arrived. They walked through the front door with champagne and arrogance, unaware that the 'Orphan' they despised was waiting to lead them to the gallows.
Seo-Jun "Let them in, Lisa. The 'Audit' is about to become a Board Meeting. And this time... everyone is going to pay their debts."
