CHAPTER 21: THE ADOPTED RULE
KIM MANSION // THE GRAND FOYER // 08:30 AM
The house had transformed. It was no longer a home; it was a hunting ground. Up on the balcony, May and Unisan were huddled together. Their world had shattered. They weren't daughters of a President; they were "assets" of a Dynasty. They watched me—the boy who had shared their table—knowing that I now owned their futures.
BOARD LEADERSHIP: IDENTIFIED

Shefiee, the Chairperson of the Board, is the architect of the coup. Her wealth rivals the Kims, and her influence over the other members—the weak-willed directors—is absolute. She believes in "Purity" and "Traditional Values," making the footage of her daughter Rhea even more lethal.

President Kim "She's here. Look at her, Seo-Jun. Look at that arrogance."
A black Rolls Royce pulled into the driveway. A woman stepped out, draped in Chanel, her eyes sharp enough to cut glass. Shefiee. She walked toward the front door as if she already owned the deed to the mansion.
President Kim "Go to the door, Heir. Greet the woman who wants to destroy us. Don't hide the marks on your neck. Let her smell her daughter's scent on you. We are going to have a 'Brunch' she will never forget."
I stepped toward the heavy oak doors. I could hear Lisa sobbing in the kitchen, and I could feel May’s eyes burning into my back. I straightened my suit, my pulse steady. I wasn't the adopted orphan anymore. I was the wolf the President had built.
