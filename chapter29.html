CHAPTER 29: THE BLOOD WEDDING AUDIT
The heavy mahogany doors of the President’s study didn’t just open; they were conquered. Seo-Jun entered first, his stride radiating the calm of a man who had already won. Behind him, close enough to feel his shadow, was Hana—her expression unreadable, her loyalty no longer for sale.
President Kim "You’re late for the final report, Seo-Jun. And I see you’ve brought my shadow with you. Tell me... did she finish the 'Audit' of your neck?"
** THUD — THE KNIFE HITS THE DESK **
Seo-Jun didn't speak. He reached into his pocket and produced the master override device Hana had surrendered. He placed it directly atop the President’s legal files, his hand resting firmly over it—a mirrored echo of how he had pinned Hana’s hand only moments ago.
Seo-Jun "The Audit is complete, Father. But not of the Board. I audited your succession plan. You wanted to see which of us was the better tool? You made a mistake. You forgot that a tool is only useful as long as the master can hold it."
Hana "He didn't break my grip, Dad. He took the lead. The security codes are now synced to his biometric signature. The guards don't report to you anymore. They report to the House of Kim... and the House has a new head."
SITUATION: TOTAL COUPE D'ÉTAT

The alliance is verified. By merging Seo-Jun's legal legitimacy with Hana's tactical control, they have effectively removed the President from his own network. The "Marriage" is now the only law in the mansion.

The President looked at them—the Blood Heir and the Steel Weapon—standing in perfect, lethal sync. For the first time in thirty years, the man who controlled the empire felt the cold draft of his own irrelevance.
Seo-Jun "We aren't here to ask for the throne. We're here to inform you that the Audit found you obsolete. Sign the abdication, or Hana will demonstrate exactly how 'precise' your greatest weapon can be when she's aiming for the creator."
