CHAPTER 23: THE BLOODLINE LEGACY
KIM MANSION // THE DINING HALL // THE STANDOFF
The atmosphere in the room didn't just chill; it died. Shefiee, who had just laughed off the footage of Rhea, froze as Gobo stepped into the light. His presence wasn't that of a 'best friend'—it was the shadow of a past Shefiee thought she had buried under a mountain of Board votes.
Shefiee (Voice trembling) "Gobo... what are you doing in this house? This is a private matter for the Board. Get out before I have you erased."
Gobo "Erased? Like you erased your sister thirteen years ago? The one who supposedly died in a 'tragic accident'... the same accident that left Seo-Jun an orphan?"
** GASP - SHATTER - SILENCE **
Seo-Jun felt the world tilt. He looked from Gobo to President Kim. The 'Masterpiece of Fiction' his father mentioned in the kitchen... the layers were deeper than he imagined.
President Kim "Tell her, Gobo. Tell the Chairperson what we found in the basement of her old estate. The 'Purity' she preaches is built on a foundation of corpses."
Gobo "Rhea isn't just your niece, Shefiee. She’s the proof of your betrayal. You raised her to keep her quiet. Because if she knew that you were the one who cut the brake lines on her mother's car... your 'Traditional Values' would look like a death sentence."
THE ULTIMATE LEVERAGE

The footage of Rhea wasn't the end. It was the lure. The real weapon is the truth about the Bloodline. Shefiee killed her sister to take control of the Board—and Seo-Jun's father knew it all along.

Shefiee collapsed back into her chair. Her 'Chanel Armor' was gone. She looked at Seo-Jun—the man who had ruined her 'Jewel'—and Gobo—the man who held the key to her gallows. She was surrounded by the very 'predators' she tried to fire.
Seo-Jun (Leaning in, whispering in her ear) "Now, Chairperson... about that 'transfer of ownership.' I think you’re the one who needs to sign. Or should I call Rhea in here and tell her who really killed her mother?"
** PEN - PAPER - POWER **
Shefiee "But wait, what are the proofs? No body was ever found with a mark. The police called it a tragic failure of machinery. So, unless you have the ghost of my sister standing behind you, or a piece of evidence that has survived thirteen years of my 'cleanup'"
